## Persona Card Template

Use this template for each user persona in the PRD. Complete every section — empty sections indicate insufficient research.

---

# Persona Card: [Name]

**Type:** [Primary / Secondary / Anti-Persona]

## Profile

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Name** | [Fictional, realistic name] |
| **Role** | [Job title] |
| **Company** | [Company type — size, industry, stage] |
| **Experience** | [Years in role / years with product category] |
| **Technical proficiency** | [Novice / Intermediate / Advanced / Expert] |
| **Location** | [Geographic context if relevant] |

## Daily Context

**A typical day:** [2-3 sentences describing what their work day looks like, what tools they use, and what pressures they face]

**Key tools in their stack:**
- [Tool 1 — what they use it for]
- [Tool 2 — what they use it for]
- [Tool 3 — what they use it for]

## Goals

**Primary goal:** [One sentence — the thing they most want to achieve related to this initiative]

**Secondary goals:**
- [Goal 2 — supporting objective]
- [Goal 3 — supporting objective]

**What success looks like:** [How they would know the initiative has helped them — in their terms, not product metrics]

**Underlying motivation:** [The deeper why — career growth, reducing stress, serving their customers better, proving their team's value]

## Frustrations

**Current pain points:**
1. [Pain point 1 — specific and relatable]
2. [Pain point 2]
3. [Pain point 3]

**Current workarounds:**
- [What they do today to work around pain point 1]
- [What they do today to work around pain point 2]

**Cost of the status quo:** [Quantified where possible — hours wasted per week, errors per month, money spent on alternatives]

**Emotional dimension:** [How the problem makes them feel — frustrated at wasted time, anxious about errors, embarrassed when presenting inaccurate data]

## Behaviour Patterns

| Pattern | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Frequency of use** | [How often they would use this feature] |
| **Context** | [When and where — office, remote, mobile, in meetings] |
| **Decision style** | [Explorer vs. follower, impulsive vs. deliberate] |
| **Adoption pattern** | [Early adopter / Pragmatist / Sceptic] |
| **Learning preference** | [Self-serve docs, video tutorials, peer learning, training sessions] |

## Relationship to the Problem

**How directly they experience the problem:** [Directly, indirectly, or not at all (for anti-personas)]

**What would change if the problem were solved:** [Concrete description of the improvement to their daily work]

**Their ideal solution (in their words):** [What they would describe if asked "what would you want?" — not product language but user language]

## Quotes

> "[Quote about their current frustration — something they would say in an interview]"

> "[Quote about what they wish existed]"

> "[Quote about what matters most to them in any solution]"

## For Anti-Personas Only

**Why they are excluded:** [Their needs are fundamentally different / Serving them would compromise the primary persona / Their use case belongs to a different initiative]

**How to handle their requests:** [Redirect to initiative X / Defer to phase 2 / Explicitly decline with rationale]

**Risk if they are included:** [What goes wrong if you try to serve this persona alongside the primary persona]
